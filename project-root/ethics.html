Ethical Use of AI

Practical guidelines for academic integrity, verification, and fairness.

Responsible use in the academe

Ateneo de Manila University has introduced a new policy to ensure the ethical and responsible use of AI, focusing on promoting transparency, accountability, and fairness in AI technologies used within the university, while also addressing concerns such as data privacy, bias, and the potential impact on employment and academic integrity

source: https://theguidon.com/2025/03/ateneo-releases-new-policy-on-ai-to-ensure-ethical-and-responsible-use-in-the-university/

Avoiding Plagiarism & Misuse

Ensuring originality begins with clearly distinguishing between human-created and AI-generated content. All AI-assisted outputs should be documented, verified, and rewritten in the user's own voice before use. Proper attribution must be provided when referencing AI tools or external sources, and institutional policies should be followed to prevent unintentional misuse. Regular reviews and transparency in how AI is used help maintain academic and professional integrity.

source: https://contenthurricane.com/blog/genai-plagiarism/

Accuracy & verification of AI outputs

AI-generated information must always be treated as a draft, not a final source of truth. Verification involves cross-checking facts with credible references, validating numerical data, and ensuring the content aligns with current, real-world information. Human oversight is essential—evaluators should review coherence, relevance, and completeness. Maintaining updated evaluation criteria and applying critical judgment helps ensure the reliability and correctness of AI-produced content.

source: https://clarivate.com/academia-government/blog/evaluating-the-quality-of-generative-ai-output-methods-metrics-and-best-practices/

Bias and fairness

AI systems can inherit biases from skewed training data or flawed model structures, affecting decisions in areas like hiring, lending, or categorization. Ethical usage requires awareness of these potential biases, active identification of unfair patterns, and consistent testing for discriminatory outcomes. Working with diverse datasets, applying bias-detection tools, and maintaining transparency in how decisions are generated help support equitable and fair AI practices.

source: https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/artificial-intelligence/fairness-and-bias-in-artificial-intelligence/

Accessibility and Inclusivity

Ethical AI must be designed to serve users of different backgrounds, abilities, and communities. Achieving inclusivity requires building systems that are easy to use, culturally aware, and accessible to individuals with disabilities. This includes considering language diversity, assistive technologies, and user-centered design principles. Prioritizing marginalized groups and assessing real-world accessibility gaps ensures that AI tools contribute to broader social equity rather than reinforcing exclusion.

source: https://ssir.org/articles/entry/inclusive-generative-artificial-intelligence

Responsible AI Deployment

Deploying AI responsibly means maintaining transparency in how systems operate, protecting user privacy, and ensuring the technology aligns with legal and ethical standards. Clear oversight mechanisms, continuous monitoring, and regular updates help reduce risks such as misinformation, misuse, or unintended harm. Organizations should act with accountability, documenting decisions, and ensuring that AI systems remain safe, secure, and beneficial to all stakeholders.

source: https://www.iso.org/artificial-intelligence/responsible-ai-ethics

Ethical Collaboration with AI Developers

Developers and organizations must work together with a shared commitment to ethical AI design. This includes establishing guidelines for fairness, transparency, and accountability, as well as engaging in ongoing communication about risks, limitations, and societal impact. Ethical collaboration encourages continuous improvement, diverse team perspectives, responsible data handling, and a clear alignment with human rights. Through these practices, AI development becomes both trustworthy and socially responsible.

source: https://kestria.com/insights/integrating-ethical-principles-into-ai-development/
